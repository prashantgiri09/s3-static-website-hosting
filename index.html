About Me

Hello! I’m Prashant Giri, a highly motivated and enthusiastic third-year student pursuing B.E. in Computer Science Engineering at Chandigarh University. I have a deep interest in DevOps, Cloud Computing, and Infrastructure as Code (IaC), and I’m passionate about leveraging these technologies to build scalable, automated, and reliable systems.

Over the past few years, I’ve immersed myself in hands-on learning and real-world projects. I’ve worked with tools like AWS, Terraform, Docker, Jenkins, and Ansible to set up CI/CD pipelines, deploy cloud infrastructure, and automate repetitive tasks. I’ve also developed full-stack applications using Java, Spring Boot, React, Tailwind CSS, and MySQL/PostgreSQL. My projects reflect my passion for technology and problem-solving, including high availability setups on AWS, Lambda-based auto-scaling, and static website deployment with Terraform.

What drives me most is the process of continuous learning and building. I love experimenting with new tools, fixing broken configurations, and understanding systems at a deeper level. My approach combines practical knowledge with curiosity, which helps me adapt quickly and think critically under challenges.

I aim to become a skilled DevOps Engineer who can bridge the gap between development and operations through automation, cloud-native architectures, and secure deployments. I believe that technology, when used effectively, can solve real-world problems efficiently—and I want to be part of that change.

Outside of tech, I enjoy exploring new things, sharing knowledge, and staying updated with the latest trends in the DevOps and cloud ecosystem.
